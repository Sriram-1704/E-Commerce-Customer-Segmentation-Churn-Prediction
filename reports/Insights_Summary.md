### E-Commerce Customer Segmentation \& Churn Prediction



#### Overall Business Performance



The platform has 4,338 total customers, out of which 1,389 customers have churned.



The overall churn rate is 32.02%, indicating that nearly 1 in 3 customers stop engaging, which is a significant business risk.



The business generated ₹8.91M in total sales, with an average customer value of ₹2.05K.



#### Customer Segmentation (RFM Analysis)



Customers are divided into four segments based on Recency, Frequency, and Monetary value:



Segment	Percentage	Key Insight

Inactive Customers	62.9%	Largest segment, major churn contributors

Low Value Customers	18.8%	Low engagement and spending

Good Customers	12.5%	Moderate value and retention

Best Customers	5.8%	Highly valuable, loyal customers



###### Insight:

More than 80% of customers fall into Inactive and Low Value segments, highlighting poor engagement and retention.



#### Churn Analysis



Retained Customers: 2,949 (67.98%)



Churned Customers: 1,389 (32.02%)



The Inactive Customers segment has the highest churn count, followed by Low Value Customers.



Best Customers show minimal churn, proving strong loyalty and engagement.



###### Insight:

Churn is highly concentrated among low-engagement segments, not among high-value customers.



#### Churn Risk Insights



High-Risk Customers: 2,730 customers fall into high churn-risk category.



Inactive Customers have:



Highest average recency (customers have not purchased for a long time)



Lowest purchase frequency



Best Customers have:



Highest purchase frequency



Lowest churn rate



###### Insight:

Customers with high recency and low purchase frequency are most likely to churn.



## Behavioral Patterns



Average Recency: 118.91 days



Average Purchase Frequency: 4.27



Customers purchasing frequently are significantly less likely to churn.



###### Insight:

Early detection of decreasing purchase frequency can act as a churn warning signal.
